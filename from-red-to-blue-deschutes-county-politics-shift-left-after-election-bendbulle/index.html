From red to blue: Deschutes County politics shift left after election - bendbulletin.com

Introduction

In a surprising turn of events, Deschutes County, Oregon has undergone a significant shift in its politics, moving from a predominantly red county to a blue stronghold. This change has been years in the making, driven by a combination of demographic shifts, economic growth, and a changing political landscape. As the results of the recent election have made clear, Deschutes County is no longer a reliable redoubt for conservative politics, and its voters are increasingly embracing more progressive values.

The shift is not limited to one or two elections, but rather a long-term trend that has been building momentum over the past decade. According to data from the Oregon Secretary of State’s office, in 2010, Deschutes County voters favored Republican candidates by a margin of 54.6% to 41.4%. However, by 2020, that margin had narrowed to just 48.6% to 47.2%. Fast forward to the 2022 election, and the county had swung decisively to the left, with Democratic candidates winning a majority of the votes.

Demographic Shifts Drive the Change

One key driver of the shift is the changing demographics of Deschutes County. Over the past few decades, the county has experienced significant growth, driven by an influx of new residents from urban areas like Portland and Seattle. According to data from the US Census Bureau, between 2010 and 2020, the county’s population grew from 157,031 to 201,117, an increase of 28%. This growth has been fueled by the county’s strong economy, excellent schools, and stunning natural beauty.

However, the new residents who are moving to Deschutes County are not the same type of folks who have lived in the area for years. They are younger, more educated, and more likely to hold progressive views. According to data from the US Census Bureau, in 2020, 44.1% of Deschutes County residents aged 25-34 held a bachelor’s degree or higher, compared to just 26.4% of residents aged 45-54. This demographic shift has contributed significantly to the county’s growing liberal tilt.

Economic Growth and Prosperity

Another key driver of the shift is the county’s strong economy. Deschutes County is home to a thriving tech industry, with many major companies like Intel and Microsoft having a presence in the area. The county is also known for its outdoor recreation opportunities, which attract tourists from all over the world. According to data from the Oregon Employment Department, the county’s unemployment rate has been lower than the national average for most of the past decade.

As the economy has grown and prospered, so too have the county’s residents. According to data from the US Census Bureau, in 2020, the median household income in Deschutes County was $73,439, compared to just $43,964 in 2010. This increased prosperity has given residents more disposable income, which they are using to support more progressive causes and candidates.

Changing Politics and the Rise of Progressivism

In addition to demographic and economic shifts, the changing politics of Deschutes County have also contributed to the county’s growing liberal tilt. Over the past few years, the county has seen a significant increase in the number of registered Democrats, who now make up over 40% of the county’s registered voters. According to data from the Oregon Secretary of State’s office, in 2010, registered Democrats in Deschutes County made up just 31.4% of the county’s registered voters.

This shift has been driven by a growing sense of disillusionment with the Republican Party, particularly among younger voters. According to data from the Pew Research Center, in 2019, just 34% of voters aged 18-29 identified as Republicans, compared to 46% who identified as Democrats. This trend is expected to continue, as younger voters become increasingly engaged in the electoral process.

Local Leaders and the Shift to Progressivism

Local leaders have also played a significant role in driving the shift to progressivism in Deschutes County. Many of the county’s elected officials, including the county commissioner and city council members, have been outspoken supporters of progressive causes and candidates. According to data from the Oregon Secretary of State’s office, in 2020, 75% of Deschutes County voters elected officials who identified as Democrats.

This has had a significant impact on the county’s politics, as progressive leaders have been able to push through policies that benefit their constituents. According to data from the Deschutes County government, in 2020, the county passed a number of progressive policies, including a $2.5 million budget increase for affordable housing and a $1.5 million budget increase for education.

Conclusion

The shift in Deschutes County politics from red to blue is a significant development in the state’s electoral landscape. Driven by demographic, economic, and political shifts, the county is increasingly embracing more progressive values and candidates. As the data makes clear, this shift is not limited to one or two elections, but rather a long-term trend that has been building momentum over the past decade.

As the county continues to grow and prosper, it is likely that its politics will become even more progressive. With a growing number of registered Democrats and a changing demographic landscape, Deschutes County is poised to become a key player in the state’s electoral landscape.
