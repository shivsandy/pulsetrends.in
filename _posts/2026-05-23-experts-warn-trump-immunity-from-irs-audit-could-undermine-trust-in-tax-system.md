# Experts Warn Trump Immunity from IRS Audit Could Undermine Trust in Tax System

The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) plays a crucial role in ensuring the integrity of the tax system in the United States. The agency is responsible for collecting taxes, enforcing tax laws, and providing tax assistance to taxpayers. However, a recent development has raised concerns among experts that the IRS's ability to maintain public trust could be compromised. In 2020, the U.S. Treasury Department's Office of Tax Policy issued a memo stating that President Donald Trump and his family would be granted immunity from IRS audit. This move has sparked controversy, and experts warn that it could undermine trust in the tax system.

## The Importance of IRS Audits

IRS audits are a critical component of the tax system, serving as a means of ensuring that taxpayers comply with tax laws and regulations. The process involves a thorough examination of a taxpayer's financial records to verify the accuracy of their tax returns. Audits can be conducted randomly or based on specific criteria, such as a taxpayer's income level or a high-risk industry. According to the IRS, the audit rate for individual tax returns has declined significantly in recent years, with only about 0.4% of individual tax returns audited in 2020.

Despite the low audit rate, the IRS still plays a vital role in maintaining the integrity of the tax system. Audits help to prevent tax evasion, ensure compliance with tax laws, and provide a sense of security for taxpayers who are honest about their tax obligations. By providing a transparent and fair process, the IRS can instill trust among taxpayers and reinforce the public's confidence in the tax system.

## The Trump Immunity Issue

In 2020, the U.S. Treasury Department's Office of Tax Policy issued a memo stating that President Trump and his family would be granted immunity from IRS audit. This move was reportedly made in response to a request from the Trump administration. The memo, which was leaked to the press, stated that the IRS would not audit the president or his family members without the explicit consent of the Treasury Department.

Experts have expressed concerns that this move undermines the integrity of the tax system. By granting immunity to the president and his family, the IRS is effectively exempting them from the audit process. This can create a perception that the tax system is unfair and biased, undermining the public's trust in the agency.

## The Impact on Public Trust

Public trust in the tax system is essential for its effectiveness. When taxpayers feel that the system is fair and transparent, they are more likely to comply with tax laws and regulations. Conversely, when they perceive the system as unfair or biased, they may be more likely to evade taxes or seek ways to avoid paying their fair share.

A survey conducted by the Pew Research Center in 2020 found that 63% of Americans believed that the tax system is unfair, while 71% believed that the tax system favors the wealthy. These sentiments are likely to worsen if the IRS is seen as favoring certain individuals or groups, such as the president and his family.

## AI and Tax Enforcement

Artificial intelligence (AI) is increasingly being used by the IRS to enhance tax enforcement and compliance. AI-powered systems can analyze large datasets, identify patterns, and detect anomalies in taxpayer behavior. This can help the IRS to target high-risk taxpayers and identify potential tax evasion.

However, the use of AI in tax enforcement also raises concerns about bias and fairness. If AI systems are not designed and trained properly, they may perpetuate existing biases and discrimination. This can exacerbate the perception that the tax system is unfair and biased.

## Conclusion

The decision to grant President Trump and his family immunity from IRS audit has sparked controversy and raised concerns about the integrity of the tax system. Experts warn that this move could undermine public trust in the IRS and the tax system as a whole. The use of AI in tax enforcement also raises concerns about bias and fairness. To maintain public trust, it is essential that the IRS ensures that its processes are transparent, fair, and unbiased.
